READ-ONLY AUDIT ONLY — Broker Compensation + Agency Agreement Terms + Services Structure

GOAL:
Audit Offer Presets and Offer pages to confirm all Broker Compensation & Agency Agreement Terms and Services match the correct user role and property type.

DO NOT EDIT ANYTHING.
DO NOT CHANGE CODE.

AUDIT SCOPE:
Offer Presets only:
- Buyer
- Seller
- Landlord
- Tenant

Property types:
- Residential
- Income
- Commercial
- Business Opportunity
- Vacant Land
- Commercial Leasing where applicable

AUDIT PART 1 — Broker Compensation & Agency Agreement Terms

For each role + property type, identify:
1. Which compensation fields are shown
2. Which agency agreement fields are shown
3. Which options are shown under each field
4. Which fields are incorrectly shared across roles/property types
5. Whether residential is showing commercial options
6. Whether commercial is showing residential options
7. Whether “Other, Specify” fields only appear when Other is selected
8. Whether labels match the intended wording

Pay special attention to:
- Buyer compensation terms
- Seller compensation terms
- Landlord residential lease fee terms
- Landlord commercial lease fee terms
- Tenant lease fee terms
- Lease-option fee terms
- Retainer fees
- Protection periods
- Early termination fees
- Payment Timing for Broker Fees
- Referral Fee placement

EXPECTED RULE:
Broker Compensation terms must be role-specific and property-type-specific.
Do not mix Buyer/Seller/Landlord/Tenant terms.
Do not mix Residential/Commercial/Income/Business/Vacant Land terms.

AUDIT PART 2 — Services Structure

For each role + property type, identify:
1. Current services shown
2. Whether services are grouped by category or displayed as one long list
3. Whether service categories match the listing creation format
4. Whether custom services save
5. Whether custom services display on Offer page
6. Whether services appear read-only on public/review pages
7. Whether services become editable only in bid/counter/negotiation workflows

EXPECTED RULE:
Services should match the same category-based format used in listing flows, not appear as one unorganized list.

Example desired structure:
- Marketing & Promotion
- Search / Property Matching
- Showings / Tours
- Application or Offer Support
- Negotiation Support
- Contract / Lease / Closing Support
- Move-In or Transaction Coordination
- Additional Services / Custom Services

AUDIT PART 3 — File Map

Identify the exact files/components/controllers/models responsible for:

1. Offer Preset save scope logic
2. Offer Preset role/property type detection
3. Broker Compensation field definitions/options
4. Agency Agreement field definitions/options
5. Services field definitions/categories
6. Custom Services save logic
7. Custom Services display logic
8. Offer page services display
9. Bid/counter services edit display
10. Referral Fee display/location logic

AUDIT PART 4 — Source-of-Truth Risk

Determine whether the system currently has:
1. One shared source of truth for services and compensation fields
2. Duplicated arrays/options across multiple files
3. Hardcoded role/property options in Blade views
4. Role/property conditionals scattered across many files
5. Legacy labels such as “Split Payment Due”
6. Commercial fields leaking into residential
7. Residential fields leaking into commercial
8. Referral Fee mixed into Broker Compensation
