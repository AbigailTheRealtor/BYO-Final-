FINAL LAUNCH AUDIT — Offer Listing Terms vs Listing Data Entry Forms

Context:
We are preparing for launch and need a read-only audit first. Do NOT make code changes in this task unless separately approved.

Goal:
Audit our current Full Service Offer Listing create/edit flows against the uploaded MLS-style data entry forms and contract/offer-term source documents.

The purpose is to separate:
1. Listing/property data
2. Negotiated offer terms
3. Contract/addendum terms
4. Brokerage compensation/agency terms

IMPORTANT:
This audit is NOT for AI Offer Comparison, AI scoring, rankings, weighting, offer analysis, or match score logic.
AI Offer Comparison will be handled in a completely separate future audit.

This is NOT a task to add every MLS field.
We only need to confirm that our actual Offer Listing forms contain the correct negotiated terms by:
- user role
- property type
- transaction type

Hard Safeguards:
- READ-ONLY AUDIT ONLY.
- Do NOT modify files.
- Do NOT change database schema.
- Do NOT create migrations.
- Do NOT change validation.
- Do NOT change routes.
- Do NOT touch Limited Service; Limited Service is legacy/frozen.
- Do NOT touch Hire Agent listing flows.
- Only audit Full Service Offer Listing flows.
- Do NOT remove or rename existing fields.
- Do NOT alter draft, submit, bid, counter, accepted-summary, or PDF logic.
- If a needed field is missing, document it only.
- If a field appears in the wrong role/property type, document it only.
- Stop and report findings before making any fix.

Audit Method:
For each Full Service Offer Listing role/property type, classify each current field into one of these categories:

1. Listing Metadata
Examples:
address, property type, beds, baths, sqft, zoning, year built, lot size, utilities, water access, dock, parking, appliances, photos, public remarks.

2. Negotiated Offer Terms
Examples:
purchase price, lease price, deposits, escrow, closing date, occupancy, inspection period, financing type, seller concessions, lease term, rent amount, renewal option, purchase option, lease-option fee, CAM/NNN/pass-through terms, seller financing, exchange/trade, assignment, contingencies.

3. Contract/Addendum Terms
Examples:
HOA/condo, flood, lead-based paint, survey, environmental, septic/sewer, feasibility/due diligence, tenant estoppels, lease transfer, association approval, right of first refusal.

4. Brokerage Terms
Examples:
buyer broker commission, tenant broker commission, broker compensation structure, agency relationship, protection period, retainer/termination fee.

Main Audit Questions:
For every role/property type, answer:

A. Are we missing any essential negotiated offer terms?
B. Are we showing listing metadata where negotiated terms should appear?
C. Are any fields showing for the wrong property type?
D. Are any Seller-only terms showing to Buyer/Tenant/Landlord incorrectly?
E. Are any Lease terms showing in Purchase-only flows incorrectly?
F. Are Commercial Lease economics complete enough for launch?
G. Are Business Opportunity negotiated terms complete enough for launch?
H. Are Income Property investor terms complete enough for launch?
I. Are Vacant Land feasibility/due diligence terms complete enough for launch?
J. Are Lease-Option / Lease-Purchase terms complete enough for launch?
K. Are conditional triggers correct based on property type, financing type, lease type, and transaction type?
