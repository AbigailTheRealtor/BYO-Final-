# Offer System Governance

## Purpose

The Offer System extends the Bid Your Offer platform to support formal, structured real estate offers between buyers/tenants and sellers/landlords. It provides a transparent, auditable workflow for submitting, countering, comparing, and accepting offers — building on top of the existing auction bid infrastructure without replacing it.

The Offer System is designed to:
- Give parties a structured, documented channel for negotiating real property transactions.
- Surface offer terms in a machine-readable format that can be compared, archived, and later analysed.
- Reduce ambiguity by capturing key deal terms (price, contingencies, timelines, compensation) in standardised fields.
- Provide a clear audit trail from initial offer through counter-offers to acceptance.

## Legal / Compliance Guardrails

### No Legal Advice
The platform does not provide legal advice. Nothing generated, displayed, summarised, or recommended by the Offer System — including AI-generated explanations, offer comparisons, compatibility scores, or acceptance guidance — constitutes legal advice. Users are solely responsible for understanding and agreeing to the terms of any offer or counter-offer before signing or accepting.

### No AI Acceptance or Rejection Decisions
AI features within the Offer System (Phase 7 and beyond) are limited to **explanation and analysis only**. AI must never:
- Accept or reject an offer on behalf of a user.
- Recommend a specific acceptance or rejection without explicit human review and confirmation.
- Make binding commitments, issue counter-offers, or modify offer terms autonomously.

All acceptance, rejection, and counter-offer actions must be performed by an authenticated human user via explicit UI actions.

### Accepted Offer Summary Is Not a Final Contract
An "Accepted Offer Summary" generated by this system is a **structured record of agreed terms** — it is not a legally binding contract unless and until it is:
1. Reviewed and signed by all parties through an approved legal or electronic-signature workflow (e.g., a compliant e-sign integration introduced in Phase 6), **and**
2. Handled in accordance with applicable state and local real estate transaction laws.

The platform must display a clear disclaimer whenever an Accepted Offer Summary is shown or exported.

### User Responsibility
Users remain responsible for:
- Reading and understanding all offer terms before accepting or countering.
- Consulting a licensed real estate professional, attorney, or broker as required by their jurisdiction.
- Ensuring that any accepted offer meets state-specific real estate disclosure, contract, and agency requirements.

### State-Specific Requirements
Real estate transactions are governed by state law. Requirements for disclosures, agency relationships, earnest money handling, contract form, and attorney involvement vary by state. The platform does not validate or enforce state-specific requirements. Users must seek guidance from a licensed professional in their state.

### Broker and Platform Liability
The platform acts as a technology intermediary only. It does not act as a broker, agent, or fiduciary for any party. The platform and its operators bear no liability for:
- The terms of any offer, counter-offer, or accepted summary.
- Losses arising from a failed transaction, misunderstood terms, or a dispute between parties.
- The legal sufficiency of any document generated by the system.

### AI Explanation Boundaries
AI outputs are advisory only. AI features (Phase 7) may:
- Explain what a specific offer field means in plain language.
- Summarise the differences between two offers or between an offer and a counter-offer.
- Flag missing or unusual fields for human review.

AI features must not:
- Determine offer rankings automatically.
- Determine offer winners or recommend a specific offer for acceptance.
- Determine the legal sufficiency of any offer, counter-offer, or accepted summary.
- Replace broker judgment.
- Replace attorney review.
- Execute negotiations, issue counter-offers, or modify offer terms independently.
- Negotiate terms on behalf of a user.
- Represent itself as a licensed agent, attorney, or broker.
- Generate legal documents (e.g., purchase agreements, lease agreements) without explicit legal/e-sign integration oversight.

## Fair Housing Compliance

The Offer System — including all AI analysis, offer comparison views, compatibility scoring, Property DNA, Buyer DNA, Tenant DNA, and any recommendation or ranking features — must not consider, reference, or produce outputs that reflect protected classes.

No AI output, compatibility score, or offer ranking may recommend, prioritise, discourage, or rank participants based on:
- Race
- Color
- Religion
- Sex
- Disability
- Familial status
- National origin
- Or any other protected class under applicable federal, state, or local fair housing law.

This constraint applies to all phases. Any new AI prompt, scoring model, or comparison feature introduced in any phase must be reviewed against this requirement before deployment. Features that cannot be demonstrated to be free of protected-class influence must not be released.

---

## Change Control

Changes to the Offer System that affect offer state transitions, accepted offer storage, PDF output, or e-sign integration must be reviewed against these guardrails before implementation. No phase may introduce autonomous decision-making that bypasses the human-confirmation requirement.
